# How to Choose Your Domain Name: The Complete Guide

Your domain name is your permanent digital address. Unlike most website decisions, it's hard to change later — so getting it right the first time matters.

## The Golden Rules of Domain Name Selection

### 1. Keep It Short and Memorable
Under 15 characters is ideal. The shorter the domain, the easier it is to type, remember, and share verbally. Amazon, Google, Stripe — all short, distinct, memorable.

### 2. Make It Easy to Spell
If people can't spell it from hearing it, you'll lose traffic. Avoid unusual spellings, hyphens, and numbers.

### 3. Avoid Hyphens and Numbers
- "best-web-agency-paris.com" is forgettable and looks spammy
- Numbers create ambiguity ("is it 5 or five?")

### 4. Choose the Right Extension
- **.com** — universal first choice; most trusted globally
- **.fr** — strong signal for French local businesses; builds trust with French consumers
- **.io** — popular for tech startups and SaaS
- **.me**, **.co**, **.agency** — alternatives when .com is taken

For a French local business targeting French customers, a .fr domain paired with a .com is ideal.

### 5. Avoid Trademark Issues
Before registering, search the INPI (French trademark office) and Google your chosen name to ensure it's not already in use by another business in your sector.

## Should Your Domain Name Include Keywords?

In the early days of SEO, keyword-rich domains (bestparisplumber.com) had ranking advantages. Today, that advantage is minimal and the negatives (hard to remember, brand confusion) outweigh the benefits.

**Better approach:** choose a brandable name (your business name, a coined word, an abbreviation) and build your SEO through quality content.

## Where to Register Your Domain

Recommended registrars:
- **OVH** — French leader, competitive pricing
- **Gandi** — excellent reputation, ethical practices
- **Namecheap** — affordable, good interface
- **Google Domains / Squarespace Domains** — simple if you're using their platforms

Typical pricing: €10–15/year for .com, €8–12/year for .fr.

**Tip:** Register both .com and .fr versions of your domain name if your budget allows — it protects your brand.

## Domain Name and Email

Your domain name also determines your professional email address. "contact@yourbusiness.com" is far more professional than "yourbusiness@gmail.com." Once you register your domain, set up professional email through Google Workspace (€5.75/user/month) or Microsoft 365.

## What to Do If Your Preferred Domain Is Taken

1. Try a different extension (.fr if .com is taken, or vice versa)
2. Add a small, natural modifier: "studio" + your name, or your location
3. Contact the current owner through WHOIS — many domains are registered but not actively used and the owner may sell
4. Use a domain auction platform (Sedo, Afternic) to buy premium domains

## Domain Name and SEO

Your domain name has minimal direct SEO impact today. Focus on:
- Choosing something memorable that encourages links and shares
- Keeping your domain age in mind — older domains have accumulated authority; buy an aged domain in your niche if the option exists
- Avoiding domains with a history of spam (check with mxtoolbox.com or Google "site:yourdomain.com")
