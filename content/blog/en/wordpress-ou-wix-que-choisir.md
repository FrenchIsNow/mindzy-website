# WordPress or Wix: Which One Should You Choose?

WordPress powers over 43% of the internet. Wix has over 230 million registered users. Both claim to be the best solution for building a website. So which one is actually right for you?

The honest answer: it depends on your goals, technical skills, and how much you care about long-term flexibility.

## What Is Wix?

Wix is an all-in-one hosted website builder. You choose a template, drag and drop elements, publish — no server, no plugin management, no technical maintenance. Everything is handled by Wix.

### Wix strengths

- **Extremely easy to use** — no technical knowledge required
- **Everything included** — hosting, SSL, basic SEO tools
- **Fast to launch** — a simple site in hours
- **Reliable uptime** and automatic updates

### Wix limitations

- **Limited customisation** — you're working within Wix's design system
- **Harder to scale** — complex features (membership sites, advanced e-commerce) hit limits quickly
- **Your data lives in Wix** — migrating away is painful
- **SEO ceiling** — Wix has improved significantly, but WordPress still has the edge for serious SEO

## What Is WordPress?

WordPress.org is an open-source CMS you install on your own hosting. It powers everything from simple blogs to Netflix-scale platforms.

### WordPress strengths

- **Unlimited flexibility** — 60,000+ plugins, any feature imaginable
- **SEO powerhouse** — Yoast, Rank Math, full technical control
- **You own everything** — your data, your code, your hosting
- **Massive community** — problems are always solved quickly

### WordPress limitations

- **Steeper learning curve** — you'll need to manage hosting, updates, security
- **Requires more time** — or a developer/agency to maintain correctly
- **Plugin bloat risk** — too many plugins slow sites down

## GEO Optimisation

For visibility in AI-powered search engines (Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT Search, Perplexity), your content structure matters more than your platform. That said, WordPress gives you finer control over structured data (Schema.org markup), breadcrumbs, and semantic HTML — all of which AI engines use to understand and cite your content.

If GEO is part of your strategy, WordPress with a good SEO plugin gives you more levers to pull. Wix has added some structured data support but still lags behind.

## Which Should You Choose?

**Choose Wix if:**
- You need a simple 5–10 page site quickly
- You're not technical and don't want to think about maintenance
- Your budget is tight and you're in the early stages

**Choose WordPress if:**
- You're serious about SEO and organic traffic
- You plan to build a blog, e-commerce, or membership site
- You want full control and long-term flexibility

**Work with an agency if:**
- You want the best of both — a WordPress site built correctly without the technical headache
